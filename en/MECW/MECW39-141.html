Letter to Joseph G. Mayer, after February 3, 1853

Author(s) Karl Marx
Written 3 February 1853


First published in: Marx and Engels, Works, Second Russian Edition, Vol. 50, Moscow, 1981
Published in English for the first time in Marx-Engels Collected Works, Volume 39

MARX TO J. G. MAYER[1]

IN LONDON

[London, after 3 February 1853]

Charles Williams presents his compliments to Mr Mayer and he[2]takes the liberty to transmit him the enclosed letter for Mr Szemere.

  1. ↑ Marx made this rough note in English on J. G. Mayer's letter of 3 February 1853. Marx used the pseudonym Charles Williams when he sent his letters to Szemere in Paris c/o Mayer
  2. ↑ Here the words 'will be much obliged' are crossed out in the original.
