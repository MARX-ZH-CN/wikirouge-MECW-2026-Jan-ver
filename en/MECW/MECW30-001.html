A Contribution to the Critique of Political Economy (Manuscripts of 1861-63)

Author(s) Karl Marx
Written August 1861



A Contribution to the Critique of Political Economy. Third Chapter, Capital in General
Written between August 1861 and July 1863.
Consisting of 23 notebooks with on-going pagination (overall volume: about 1,472 large pages)
Published in MECW volumes 30, 31, 32 and 34 (1988-1994), in the order of the manuscripts (that also contains the Theories of Surplus Value), not in the compiled version below.
    • 1) Transformation of Money into Capital
    • 2) Absolute Surplus Value
    • 3) Relative Surplus Value
    • 3) Relative Surplus Value (continued)
    • 3) Relative Surplus Value (end)
    • 4) Reconversion of Surplus Value into Capital
    • Capital and Profit
    • Miscellanea
    • Mercantile Capital

Marx intitled the manuscript A Contribution to the Critique of Political Economy. Third Chapter, Capital in General, since the first published part contained Chapter One. The Commodity and Chapter Two. Money or Simple Circulation.
