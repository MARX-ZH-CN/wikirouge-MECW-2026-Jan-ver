Letter to Ernst Dronke, November 20, 1877

Author(s) Friedrich Engels
Written 20 November 1877


First published in: Marx and Engels, Works, Second Russian Edition, Vol. 34, Moscow, 1964
Published in English for the first time in Marx-Engels Collected Works, Volume 45


ENGELS TO ERNST DRONKE

IN WATERLOO

[Copy]

[London], 20 November [1877]

Unless I have the RECEIPT by tomorrow morning, I shall feel compelled to go to the CENTRAL OFFICE here and inform them of everything that has taken place.[1]

  1. ↑ A reference TO ERNST DRONKE's infringement of the contract under which Engels rendered him financial assistance (see Engels' letters to Emil Blank of 16 October and TO ERNST DRONKE of 20 October and 1 and 13 November 1876 in this volume, pp. 160, 161, 164-65, 171).
