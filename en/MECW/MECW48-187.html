Letter to Eleanor Marx-Aveling, May 13, 1889

Author(s) Friedrich Engels
Written 13 May 1889


First published in English in: F. Engels, P. et L. Lafargue, Correspondance, t. II, Paris, 1956
Reproduced from the original
Published in English in Marx-Engels Collected Works, Volume 48


ENGELS TO ELEANOR MARX-AVELING[1]

IN LONDON

[London, about 13 May 1889]

As Laura has sent her letter to you in open envelope, I enclose this.[2]

Shall see you tonight at Sam's.[3]

  2. ↑ The text of this letter was written on the last page of Laura Lafargue's letter to Engels of 12 May 1889. L. Lafargue said in her letter she had been unable yet to persuade Vaillant and others to send letters to the newspaper The Star; she expressed her doubts about the expedience of such a mode of procedure. Engels replied on 14 May (see the next letter).
