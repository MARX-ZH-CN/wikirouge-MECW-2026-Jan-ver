Letter to Gottfried Kinkel, July 22, 1852

Author(s) Karl Marx
Written 22 July 1852


First published in MEGA, Abt. III, Bd. 1, 1929
Published in English for the first time in Marx-Engels Collected Works, Volume 39


MARX TO GOTTFRIED KINKEL[1]

IN LONDON [Rough copy]

[London,] 22 July 1852 5 Sutton Street, Soho Office of The People's Paper To Dr J. G. Kinkel

You are alleged, or so I am informed, to have ventured the following statement before Anneke or others at Cincinnati: 'Marx and Engels...'

a the young - b F. Smitt, Geschichte des polnischen Aufstandes und Krieges in den Jahren 1830 und 1831. c L. Mieroslawski, Kritische Darstellung des Feldzuges vom Jahre 1831. f Ernst Dronke

I await your answer by return of post. Silence will be regarded as an admission.

Dr K. M.

  1. ↑ Marx's letter to Kinkel is published according to Marx's letter to Engels of 6 August 1852 (see this volume, pp. 149-50). Marx also quotes it in his letter to Cluss of 30 July 1852 (see ibid., pp. 139, 140)
