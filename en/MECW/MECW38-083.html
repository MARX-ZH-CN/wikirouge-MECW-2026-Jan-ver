Letter to Hermann Brehmer, May 6, 1849

Author(s) Karl Marx
Written 6 May 1849


Source: Marx-Engels Collected Works, Volume 38, p. 196;
First published: in G. Becker, Neue Dokumente von Karl Marx aus dem jahre 1849, Zeitschrift für Geschichtswissenschaft, 1974.
Collection(s): Zeitschrift für Geschichtswissenschaft

Harburg, 6 May 1849

To Mr Brehmer in Breslau

I warmly recommend to you the bearer of these lines, Mr Bruhn, whose name you will still recall from the events in Southern Germany last year.[1]

Yours very truly

Karl Marx

  1. ↑ Karl Bruhn participated in the Baden republican uprising in April 1849 and played an active role in the popular uprising in Frankfurt am Main (September 1848) in protest against the ratification by the Frankfurt National Assembly of the capitulatory truce of Malmö. Concluded between Prussia and Denmark, this truce preserved Danish rule in Schleswig-Holstein. Since the end of 1848 Bruhn had been working in Hamburg and Schleswig-Holstein on the instruction of the Communist League and sending reports to the Neue Rheinische Zeitung from there.
