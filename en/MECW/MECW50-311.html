Letter to Bolesaw Jedrzejowski, June 28, 1895

Author(s) Friedrich Engels
Written 28 June 1895


First published, in the language of the original (German), in: Z pola walki, No. 4 (12), 1960
Printed according to the original
Published in English for the first time in Marx-Engels Collected Works, Volume 50


ENGELS TO BOLESLAW ANTONI JEDRZEJOWSKI

IN LONDON

Eastbourne, 28 June 1895
4 Royal Parade

Dear Comrade,

In reply to your inquiry of the 25th of this month, which has been forwarded to me here, I beg to inform you that I should have no objection at all to your translating my article in the Commonwealth and publishing it in the way you suggest.[1]

Yours faithfully, F. Engels

B. A. Jedrzejowski, Editor, Przedswit

  1. ↑ The reference is to Engels' work What Have the Working Classes to Do with Poland (present edition, Vol. 20) which, translated into Polish, was published by the journal Przedswit (No. 7) in July 1895 under the title: Klasa robotnicza a rwestya polska.
