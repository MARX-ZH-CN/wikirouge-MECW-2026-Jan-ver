Author(s) Friedrich Engels
Written 3 September 1887


First published in: Marx and Engels, Works, First Russian Edition, Vol. XXVII, Moscow, 1935

Translated from the French

ENGELS TO PYOTR LAVROV[1]

IN PARIS

London, 3 September 1887

Danielson in St Petersburg has informed me of H. Lopatin's death.[2]

Have you had confirmation? I should like to think there has been some mistake.[3]If by mischance the news should be true, let me know and I shall at once send a tribute* to the S.D. of 2[4]

Yours ever RE.

  1. ↑ Engels wrote this letter on a post card. He indicated the address on the reverse side: Monsieur P. Lavroff, 32 rue S. Jacques, Paris, France.
  2. ↑ This sentence was in Russian in the original
  3. ↑ The report about G. Lopatin's death proved wrong.
  4. ↑ Sozialdemokrat of Zürich (in the original Engels uses the Russian initials)
