ENGELS TO LAURA LAFARGUE

IN LE PERREUX

London, 26 July 1892

My dear Laura,

I receive this minute, 9.45 evening—Sonnenschein's account which results in £5.9.2., 3/5-ths of which go to Mohr's heirs, that is £ 1.1.10 each, of which cheque enclosed.

I hear from Tussy that you are very much out of sorts just now, hope you will mend soon!

To-morrow I am off to Ryde for a fortnight,[1]address:

F.E.

The Firs, Brading Road, Ryde

Louise left on Sunday for Austria.

Ever yours,

F. Engels

  1. ↑ Engels came to Ryde for a holiday on 27 July 1892. Illness made him stay on until 6 September.
