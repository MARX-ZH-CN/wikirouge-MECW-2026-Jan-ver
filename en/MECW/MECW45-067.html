Letter to Bernhard Kraus, October 20, 1875

Author(s) Karl Marx
Written 20 October 1875


First published in Weg und Ziel, Nr. 5, Wien, Mai 1978. Printed according to the original. Published in English for the first time in Marx-Engels Collected Works, Volume 45


MARX TO BERNHARD KRAUS[1]

IN VIENNA

London, 20 October 1875

Dear Friend,

You will have seen from my daughter's[2]letter that, of the 3 letters you wrote me, not one has reached London, and that the same thing happened to the letters I wrote 134 and those directed to me (during my stay at Karlsbad). This bears the stamp of—Stieber! So in future write to me at the following address: Mr John Withers, 54 Grafton Terrace, Maitland Park, London.

If I didn't write to you from Karlsbad, it was because your friends there told me that you intended to return thither from Gmünd.

With sincere regards, Your

K. M.

  1. ↑ This letter of Marx's was written on the back of Eleanor Marx's letter TO BERNHARD KRAUS of 20 October 1875.
